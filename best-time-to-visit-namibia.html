Month-by-month seasonality for Namibia: weather, crowds, prices, and what to do—plus quick recommendations for first-time trips.

Quick recommendation

Best all-round months: May–Oct. Best value: Apr/May or Sep/Oct.

Your ideal window depends on activities (safari, beaches, hiking) and tolerance for heat/rain.

Weather overview

Namibia’s dry season is ideal for road trips and wildlife viewing. Nights can be cold in winter; pack layers for desert mornings.

Wildlife and activity notes

  • Peak conditions usually align with the driest months, when animals concentrate near water and roads are easiest.
  • Green season can be excellent for scenery and photography—often with fewer crowds and better prices.
  • Heat management: plan dawn/late-afternoon activities and reserve midday for museums, cafés, or rest.

Crowds and prices

School holidays and peak safari seasons tend to push prices up. If you want a quieter trip, aim for shoulder months and book key transport early.

Practical packing checklist

  • Light layers + a warmer layer for evenings
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Closed-toe shoes for dust and walking days
  • Light rain layer if traveling in wetter months
