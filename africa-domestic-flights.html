Africa Domestic Flights

How domestic flights work across Africa: booking, baggage limits, delays, airport logistics, and connection planning.

Last updated: Feb 2026

What to expect on domestic routes

Domestic flights are often the most time-efficient way to cover large distances, but they come with practical constraints.

Aircraft can be smaller, baggage rules can be stricter, and timetables can change—especially in shoulder seasons.

  • Check baggage weight and dimensions before you arrive.
  • Arrive early for airports with manual check-in.
  • Expect schedule changes; keep an email/SMS trail.

Baggage rules and common fees

Many carriers enforce weight limits tightly. If you’re heading to safari airstrips, soft-sided bags may be required.

Budget for extra fees and keep a luggage strategy that works across all legs of your trip.

  • Carry essentials in a small day bag.
  • Use compression and a luggage scale.
  • Keep batteries and valuables in carry-on.

Connections and delays

Avoid tight domestic-to-international connections. If a missed connection would be expensive, overnight in the hub city.

When booking separate tickets, treat them as separate trips: your onward carrier won’t protect you if the first flight slips.

  • Aim for 4–8 hours buffer on separate tickets.
  • Consider arriving the day before an international flight.
  • Keep proof of onward travel if required for entry.

FAQ

How early should I arrive for a domestic flight?

Typically 2 hours is safe. For smaller airports, check local guidance and allow extra time for security/lines.

Do I need printed tickets?

Usually no, but having a PDF offline helps if mobile service is weak.

Are baggage limits strict?

Often yes—especially on smaller aircraft and safari routes.

Should I overnight in a hub before my long-haul flight?

If the long-haul is important, yes. It reduces missed-connection risk.
