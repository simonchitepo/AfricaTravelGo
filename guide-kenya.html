Kenya travel guide

Kenya combines iconic savannah safaris with a warm Indian Ocean coastline. It’s one of the easiest places to plan a first safari thanks to strong operator choice and well-established routes.

Best time to visit

For safaris, the dry months typically offer easier wildlife viewing and better road conditions. Shoulder seasons can be quieter and better value, but rain can affect game drives in some areas.

Classic 7–10 day itinerary

  • Nairobi (1–2 nights): recover from flights, museums, restaurants.
  • Maasai Mara (3–4 nights): game drives; consider a hot-air balloon add-on.
  • Amboseli or Lake Nakuru (2 nights): elephants + iconic landscapes, or rhino tracking.
  • Coast (2–4 nights): Diani, Watamu, or Lamu for slower Swahili-coast travel.

Budget planning

Safari costs are driven by park fees, vehicle/guide, and accommodation category. As a rule, private-reserve and fly-in options cost more but save time. Build a buffer for park fees, tips, and internal flights.

  • Value: group safaris + mid-range lodges.
  • Comfort: private vehicle + reputable camps.
  • Premium: conservancies, fly-in routes, and boutique camps.

Transport & booking priorities

  • Book park stays early for peak migration periods.
  • Use reputable operators; confirm what’s included (fees, meals, drinks).
  • For the coast, compare flight times vs. overland realistically.

Practical notes

  • Carry some cash for small purchases outside major cities.
  • Pack layers for early-morning game drives.

FAQ

FAQ

How many days do I need for Kenya— best time, itinerary, budget?

Most travelers get a strong first visit in 2–4 days. Add extra time if you want day trips, slower pacing, or a beach/safari extension.

What’s the best time to visit Kenya— best time, itinerary, budget?

Aim for the main dry/shoulder season to balance weather and prices. Check the page’s seasonality notes for month-by-month guidance.

Is Kenya— best time, itinerary, budget safe for tourists?

Safety is manageable with standard precautions: use registered transport at night, keep valuables low-key, and follow local guidance on neighborhoods and scams.
