Windhoek Travel Guide

Plan a trip to Windhoek, Namibia (Southern Africa): what to do, where to stay, costs, safety tips, and how to get around.

Last updated: Feb 2026

Quick overview

Windhoek is one of the standout places to visit in Namibia (Southern Africa). Use this page as a practical planning brief: what to do, how many days to allocate, how to move around, where to base yourself, and what to watch out for.

Best time: Varies by region; see guide.

Top things to do

  • craft markets
  • day trips
  • overland hub

For first-timers, prioritize the “signature” experiences above, then add one slower half‑day for neighborhoods, viewpoints, or a local market.

How many days?

Ideal: 2–4 days for most travelers.

  • 2 days: highlights + one day trip.
  • 3 days: highlights + a slower neighborhood day.
  • 4 days: add a second day trip or a nature/safari add‑on where relevant.

Getting there

Most trips start by reaching the nearest major hub (often the country’s main airport) and continuing by domestic flight, train, coach bus, shared taxi, or private transfer.

Tip: Build buffer time. Domestic schedules can shift, and overland legs can take longer than mapping apps suggest.

See: domestic flights in Africa and train & bus travel.

Getting around locally

  • Walk: best for compact historic cores and beachfront strips.
  • Ride‑hailing/taxis: confirm the fare up front where meters aren’t standard.
  • Day tours: useful for early starts, wildlife drives, or remote sights.

Safety and timing matter: avoid arriving late at night in unfamiliar areas, and plan cash/phone battery for transit days.

Where to stay

Choose a base that reduces transit friction. In many African cities, location is more important than property “star rating”.

  • Stay near your main activities (old town, beach, park gate, business district).
  • Prioritize lighting, walkability, and reliable transport access.
  • Read recent reviews for noise, water, and generator notes.

See: where to stay and Airbnb vs hotels.

Costs and budgeting

Costs vary widely by country, season, and whether you’re adding safaris or guided excursions. As a baseline, plan for:

  • Transport days: often the biggest surprise cost (fuel, transfers, baggage fees).
  • Experiences: parks, guides, or boat trips can be the “value” spend.
  • Cash buffers: ATMs can be unreliable outside major centers.

See: daily budgets by country and costs overview.

Practical tips

  • Connectivity: buy a local SIM/eSIM if you’re moving frequently. See electronics & adapters.
  • Cash: keep small denominations for tips and transport.
  • Respect: dress norms vary—especially at religious sites. See cultural etiquette.
  • Health: check recommended vaccines and malaria guidance for your route. See health & vaccines.

FAQ

How many days do I need in Windhoek?

Most travelers do well with 2–4 days: enough for the core sights plus one slower day or a day trip.

What’s the best time of year to visit Windhoek?

Varies by region; see guide.

Is Windhoek suitable for first-time visitors to Namibia?

Yes, if you plan transport days carefully, stay in well-located areas, and use reputable guides for remote activities.

What are the biggest planning mistakes to avoid?

Rushing transit days, arriving late at night without a plan, underestimating distances, and skipping a cash/phone battery buffer.
