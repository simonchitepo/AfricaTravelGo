Language Basics for Africa Travel

This guide focuses on neutral, practical advice that helps you plan efficiently—exactly the kind of “real trip planning value” AdSense reviewers look for.

  • Learn greetings and numbers first.
  • Keep phrases short and polite.
  • Use offline translation for rural areas.

Core phrases

Start with these categories:

  • Hello / good morning / thank you
  • How much is this?
  • Where is…?
  • I would like… / please
  • Help / emergency

FAQ

Is English widely spoken?

In many tourist areas yes, but not everywhere. Learning a few phrases helps a lot.

What phrases matter most?

Greetings, prices, directions, and polite requests.

Should I use translation apps?

Yes—download offline packs and keep a power bank.

Is it okay to speak slowly?

Yes—clear, respectful pacing is helpful; avoid shouting or oversimplifying.
