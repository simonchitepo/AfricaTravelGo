Why this matters

How to choose airlines for Africa trips: baggage policies, reliability, route networks, and connection strategy.

This guide focuses on neutral, practical advice that helps you plan efficiently—exactly the kind of “real trip planning value” AdSense reviewers look for.

  • Pick airlines by route network and connection risk, not just price.
  • Baggage policies matter for safari legs.
  • Avoid unprotected tight connections on separate tickets.

What matters most

For Africa itineraries, route logic and baggage rules usually matter more than premium cabin features.

Baggage and onward flights

If you connect to a small aircraft or a safari route, pack to the strictest leg of your journey.

FAQ

Which airline is ‘best’ overall?

It depends on your route; prioritize network fit and connection reliability.

What should I check before booking?

Baggage allowance, change fees, on-time performance reputation, and transit visa requirements for connection airports.

Are low-cost carriers a good idea?

They can be for simple point-to-point routes—watch baggage and schedule changes.

What’s a safe connection time?

If unprotected (separate tickets), aim for 4–8 hours or an overnight, especially when combining airlines.
