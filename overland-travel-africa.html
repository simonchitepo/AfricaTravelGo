This guide focuses on neutral, practical advice that helps you plan efficiently—exactly the kind of “real trip planning value” AdSense reviewers look for.

  • Plan shorter hops; don’t stack long transfer days.
  • Confirm departure points and schedules.
  • Prefer daylight travel and reputable operators.

Route planning principles

Use these principles:

  • Break long routes into 2–3 shorter legs.
  • Avoid backtracking by using open-jaw flights when needed.
  • Keep 10–20% of days ‘unplanned’ for delays.

FAQ

Is overland travel safe?

It can be on well-traveled routes with good operators. Timing and route choice matter most.

Should I book in advance?

For popular corridors, yes. For flexible routes, booking locally can work.

What should I pack?

Water, snacks, power bank, small cash, and offline maps.

What’s the best pacing?

Build rest days after long travel segments to reduce burnout.
