Ghana travel guide

Ghana is a friendly entry point to West Africa with a strong cultural identity: Accra’s creative scene, powerful history sites on the coast, and easy add-ons for nature and beach time.

Best time to visit

Ghana is often easiest during drier months for comfortable travel and beach time. Rainy periods can still be enjoyable but may affect road conditions in some areas.

Starter itinerary (6–9 days)

  • Accra (2–3 nights): food, art, live music, markets.
  • Cape Coast/Elmina (2 nights): history sites + coastline.
  • Kakum (day trip): canopy walk and forest scenery.
  • Optional add-on: a quieter beach stay or a cultural inland stop.

Budget and logistics

Ghana is a manageable planning destination: domestic travel is straightforward, and English is widely used. Costs vary by accommodation standard, but city weekends can be pricier.

Practical notes

  • Use trusted transport at night and plan daylight transfers when possible.
  • Bring a power bank and offline maps for long days out.

FAQ

How many days do I need for Ghana— best time, itinerary, budget?

Most travelers get a strong first visit in 2–4 days. Add extra time if you want day trips, slower pacing, or a beach/safari extension.

What’s the best time to visit Ghana— best time, itinerary, budget?

Aim for the main dry/shoulder season to balance weather and prices. Check the page’s seasonality notes for month-by-month guidance.

Is Ghana— best time, itinerary, budget safe for tourists?

Safety is manageable with standard precautions: use registered transport at night, keep valuables low-key, and follow local guidance on neighborhoods and scams.
