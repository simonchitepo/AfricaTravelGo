Where to Stay in Africa

How to choose where to stay across Africa: location strategy, safety, logistics, and what to look for in reviews.

Last updated: Feb 2026

Why this matters

How to choose where to stay across Africa: location strategy, safety, logistics, and what to look for in reviews.

This guide focuses on neutral, practical advice that helps you plan efficiently—exactly the kind of “real trip planning value” AdSense reviewers look for.

  • Pick location first (walkability/transport), then property quality.
  • Prioritize reliable water/power and recent reviews.
  • Understand neighborhood boundaries—one street can change the feel.

Location-first checklist

Use this checklist before booking:

  • How long is the commute to your key activities?
  • Is there a safe, reliable way to get back at night?
  • Are there restaurants/shops nearby?
  • Do reviews mention noise, water, or generator issues?

Safety and logistics

Safety varies by city and neighborhood. In general, minimize late-night transit, use reputable drivers, and keep arrival plans simple.

For safari areas, staying near the park gate can reduce pre-dawn travel and increase time in the field.

FAQ

Should I stay in the city center?

Often yes for convenience, but choose the safest, most walkable pockets near your priorities.

Is it better to book hotels or apartments?

Hotels can be simpler for logistics; apartments can work well in safe, well-reviewed buildings.

How do I avoid bad locations?

Cross-check maps, recent reviews, and ask the property about late arrivals and transport.

What’s the top mistake?

Booking a cheap place far from everything and spending more on transport and time.
